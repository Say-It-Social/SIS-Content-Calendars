Garba, in Bandhani.

A three‑month content strategy for a one‑day exclusive Garba event, built around the Bandhani thread — minimal, premium, rooted, and festive.

The brief, in one thread

Ber Bandhej returns as a one‑day exclusive Garba event with Bandhani as its core visual language — same as last year, but with a refreshed palette. The account should build anticipation slowly, keep the grid visually connected, and never feel loud, cluttered, or over‑produced.

This year's palette

Moving into orange

The strategy

Six threads, one campaign

One

Countdown & anticipation

Starting 30–45 days out with theme reveals, venue sneak peeks, throwback moments and countdown reels — all rooted in Bandhani — to build excitement steadily rather than all at once.

Two

The complete experience

Beyond Garba itself — food stalls, shopping zones, decor, and photo booths — so the audience reads Ber Bandhej as a full festive destination, not just a dance floor.

Three

Interactive & audience‑led

Polls, quizzes, "who's coming with you" prompts, outfit inspiration and this‑or‑that stories that keep the audience participating through the quiet weeks.

Four

Meet the performers

Singers, DJs and dancers introduced through short reels and rehearsal glimpses — a personal, behind‑the‑scenes layer, kept separate from influencer or collaboration content.

Five

Event day coverage

Live stories, crowd energy, dance highlights and an instant recap reel posted the same night — built to create FOMO and pull in last‑minute walk‑ins.

Six

The wrap‑up

Highlight reels, best‑dressed features, candid photo dumps and a thank‑you note that closes the campaign warmly and quietly opens the door to next year.

Content calendar

Three months, mapped

Month 1 — Anticipation Builds

1–2 posts / week

The Bandhani theme and this year's orange palette are introduced slowly. No performer or event‑detail content yet — this month is purely aesthetic anticipation, reminding the audience Ber Bandhej is returning, so Month 2's reveal has room to escalate.

Week 1Carousel"Ber Bandhej returns" announcement on an orange Bandhani print backdrop
Week 1StoryPoll — "Ready for Bandhej season?"
Week 2ReelThrowback edit from last year, colour‑graded warm to hint at the new palette
Week 2StoryClose‑up Bandhani dot texture in orange — "Guess the theme"
Week 3CarouselPalette reveal post — orange tones introduced formally as this year's identity
Week 3StoryThis‑or‑that — two shades of orange Bandhani
Week 4CarouselMood board — marigold, decor textures, festive details
Week 4StoryCountdown sticker — "X weeks to go"

Month 2 — Reveal & Engagement

2–3 posts / week

The event comes alive. Performers, the full experience, and interaction all enter the feed. The orange palette is now established, so posts can get specific rather than purely moody.

Week 5ReelMeet the DJ / singer — short rehearsal or intro clip, orange‑toned edit
Week 5CarouselVenue sneak peek — decor and setup teasers
Week 5StoryQuiz — "How well do you know Bandhani?"
Week 6StaticTicket / registration announcement, orange Bandhani frame
Week 6ReelDancer / performer behind‑the‑scenes glimpse
Week 6Story"Who's coming with you?" tag‑a‑friend prompt
Week 7CarouselThe full experience — food stalls, shopping zones, photo booth previews
Week 7ReelOutfit inspiration reel — orange Bandhani styling ideas
Week 7StoryPoll — "Traditional or fusion this year?"
Week 8CarouselOptional light Navratri‑context post, if it fits the aesthetic
Week 8StoryCountdown sticker + registration reminder
Of the 2–3 generic Navratri‑education posts the client is open to across the full campaign, we've placed one here — Month 2 has the room for it without disrupting the aesthetic run.

Month 3 — Countdown to Event Day

Alternate days, final 15

Frequency ramps up. The first half is final reveals and urgency — full lineup, last‑chance registration. The final 15 days shift to daily countdown content, then live event coverage, then a wrap‑up sequence that closes the campaign.

Total across the three months lands at roughly 46–48 posts, within the client's 45–50 range.
Grid direction

One connected profile, not nine separate posts

As last year, the grid is designed as a single connected layout rather than individual tiles — colour, texture and Bandhani dot placement carry across post boundaries so the profile reads as one cohesive piece when viewed as a whole.

Shot on phone throughout, no professional shoot day — planned and edited carefully enough that it doesn't need one.
