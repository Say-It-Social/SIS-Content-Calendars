01

The four benchmarks

GS
Guruprasad Srinivasan
Executive Director & Group CEO · Quess Corp
518,076 followers · BSE + NSE listed
STRONG · The credibility-plus-narrative model
What he posts
  • Financial results paired with personal career narrative in the same post — hard data + human story
  • Company milestones with "we built this together" framing — collective, not self-promotional
  • Industry recognition and apprenticeship/skill initiatives
  • Personal reflections on his 18+ year founding journey
  • Thought leadership on India's workforce formalization
The model
Pairs hard business data with an authentic personal voice. The "founding member to CEO" story is a recurring credibility anchor. Every financial post feels like a human achievement, not a press release.
Borrow: Financial milestone posts with personal framing. Collective "we built this" language. Tagging the broader leadership team.
Don't copy: Guruprasad has 18+ years of established presence. Jumping straight to financial result posts without the personal credibility layer first will land as corporate announcement. Build the personal story first (Months 1–2), then layer in IR content (Months 3+).
MS
Manish Sabharwal
Vice Chairman & Co-founder · TeamLease Services
181,463 followers · BSE + NSE listed
STRONG · The industry statesman model
What he posts
  • Co-authored opinion pieces in Economic Times and Indian Express on policy, labour codes, and FDI
  • Deep commentary on India's workforce formalization and regulatory reform
  • Wharton-trained analytical voice — writes like an economist, not a marketer
  • Lower frequency, very high quality — every post earns engagement before asking for it
The model
The gold standard for CXO thought leadership in this sector. Positioned as an industry statesman, not a company promoter. Readers follow him because they learn something they cannot get elsewhere.
Borrow: The "policy + operations" angle. Writing about India's workforce economy at the macro level. Alpit's compliance-as-moat post and Nehal's market bifurcation article are direct applications.
Don't copy: Manish writes at a policy/economist level because he sits on RBI advisory boards. RCSL's CXOs should speak from ground-operations authority — "this is what I see running operations for 20,000+ people," not "this is what India's labour policy should be."
SB
Snehashish Bhattacharjee
CEO · Denave
103,500+ followers · Private
VERY STRONG · The data-led thought leadership model
What he posts
  • Data-visual thought leadership — frameworks, metrics, outcome-led case stories
  • Case studies framed as business insights: "here's what we learned" not "here's what we did"
  • High-quality commentary on AI in sales and marketing operations
  • Consistent weekly rhythm — never sporadic
The model
Strongest content quality in the peer set. Leads with insight, not announcement. Posts earn the reader's attention before asking for anything. 103K followers built on content substance, not company scale.
Borrow: The case study format — problem → approach → measurable outcome, framed as a learning. Alpit's "from 1 client to 28 states" carousel borrows the Denave data-visual approach.
Don't copy: Denave's content is specific to sales enablement. RCSL's domain is workforce, operations, and logistics. Adopt the format, not the topics.
SIS
SIS Group Leadership
Security, Facility Mgmt & Cash Logistics · BSE + NSE listed
26,400+ LinkedIn · 48,000+ Instagram
STRONG · The operational-content model
What they post
  • Real operational content — actual sites, real teams, real equipment — not stock imagery
  • "ManTech" narrative: technology + manpower as a combined positioning
  • Team stories across the organization, not just CXO-level posts
  • Built 48K Instagram followers on operational content alone
The model
The most directly relevant benchmark for RCSL's Instagram and employer-brand play. SIS proved that operational content — the actual physical work — builds large audiences. RCSL has the same assets: warehouses, ports, fleets, teams. It just hasn't published them.
Borrow: Posting from actual sites. Alpit's "why I still visit sites" post and Jash's operations angles are direct borrowings. The ManTech narrative maps to RCSL's "we built our own tech" story.
Don't copy: SIS's 26K LinkedIn / 48K Instagram reflects years of consistent effort. Benchmark against where SIS was in its first year, not where it is now.

Short native video (60–90s)
5.5% engagement rate · 5× reach vs static posts
The 2026 algorithm update prioritizes video significantly. One short on-site or on-camera video per CXO per month. Authenticity outperforms production quality.
Caution
External links in post body
40–50% reach penalty
LinkedIn penalizes posts that push users off-platform. Never put a link in the post body. Put it in the first comment after the post goes live. This is the single most common mistake in CXO LinkedIn strategy.
Avoid
Company page reshares from personal profile
Minimal engagement · looks corporate
CXO profiles should create original content, not reshare the company page. One direction only: CXO personal post → company page amplification.

The unfair advantage competitors don't have
Quess has one active CEO voice. TeamLease has one co-founder. Denave has one CEO. SIS Group has a collective leadership presence. RCSL is putting three genuinely distinct voices on LinkedIn simultaneously — a founder-builder (Alpit), a next-generation entrepreneur (Jash), and a new professional CEO (Nehal). No single competitor has this breadth of authentic, differentiated CXO presence. If the three voices maintain their distinctiveness and consistency, RCSL's combined CXO presence should surpass SIS Group's LinkedIn footprint within 6–9 months — not by volume, but by credibility depth per follower.

How these three calendars work together

  • Three voices, not three copiesAlpit is the founder-builder. Jash is the next-gen entrepreneur. Nehal is the professional CEO. Each has a different audience, a different tone, and different content that would sound wrong from either of the other two.
  • Coordination ruleOn weeks with a company-page spotlight, each CXO references it once from their personal angle — not as a company ad, but as a genuine POV on the topic.
  • Differentiated posting daysAlpit: Mon/Wed/Fri. Jash: Tue/Thu/Sat. Nehal: Mon/Wed/Fri. Staggering avoids the three profiles posting simultaneously and maximises RCSL presence across the weekly feed.
  • IR-sensitive postsAny post touching RCSL financials, listed-company status, or forward-looking statements must be reviewed by the company secretary before publishing.
  • Personal anecdotes cannot be ghostwrittenPosts marked "Needs: CXO: personal memory..." require a real memory or data point from the individual. SIS shapes the language, but the source must come from the person.
