What Makes a Material "Truly" Biodegradable? Most Brands Get This Wrong.
✍️ Full LinkedIn Article (Summary)
If you've ever heard a supplier describe their product as "biodegradable" and felt vaguely reassured — this article is for you. The Three Categories Most People Don't Know Exist Category 1: Partially Bio-Based — Contains some plant content but blended with synthetic polymers. The natural component breaks down; the synthetic binder does not. Leaves microplastic residue. Category 2: Compostable Under Industrial Conditions — Breaks down only at temperatures above 55°C in controlled facilities. Not biodegradable in any meaningful real-world sense. Category 3: Fully Biodegradable — Breaks down in natural environmental conditions without leaving synthetic residue. This is where Green Sapio sits. 100% bio-based from source to finished product. No polyurethane binders. Verified by Eurofins. Three Questions to Ask Your Supplier: 1. Is the entire material bio-based — including binders, backings, and coatings? 2. Under what specific conditions does it biodegrade? 3. Has it been independently verified by a recognised third-party laboratory? 📩 catarun@greensapio.com · 🌐 greensapio.com #GreenSapio #Biodegradable #SustainableMaterials #ESG #CircularEconomy #BioBased #VeganLeather #Greenwashing

LinkedIn📰 Article · Monday
The EU Green Claims Directive Is Coming. Is Your Brand's Leather Story Ready?
✍️ Full LinkedIn Article (Summary)
The EU Green Claims Directive requires that any environmental claim must be substantiated by independent, scientific evidence, verified by an accredited third-party body, and specific — broad terms like "eco-friendly" or "sustainable" without qualification are prohibited. What this means for your leather: If your brand uses PU vegan leather and markets it as sustainable: this is the highest-risk position. Polyurethane is petroleum-derived, does not biodegrade, and sheds microplastics. This claim is — under the directive's framework — almost certainly unsubstantiated. A Defensible Position Looks Like: — Materials sourced from verified, renewable or recycled origins — Independent third-party testing confirming the specific claims made (e.g., Eurofins) — Claims that are specific and measurable (e.g., "87% fewer carbon emissions") — Full lifecycle transparency, including end-of-life behaviour Green Sapio's bio-based leather is designed to meet each of these criteria. 📩 catarun@greensapio.com · 🌐 greensapio.com #ESGCompliance #GreenClaimsDirective #SustainableFashion #GreenSapio #Greenwashing #EURegulation #BioBased
