📚 Myth-Busting Educational Post
Why "Vegan Leather" Isn't Always Sustainable — And What Is
✍️ Full Post Copy
The term "vegan leather" has a branding problem. When most people hear it, they imagine plant-based, natural, earth-friendly. The reality is very different. Here's the uncomfortable truth: 🔴 Over 60% of "vegan leather" is made from PU (polyurethane) — a petroleum-derived plastic. It takes 500+ years to break down. It sheds microplastics with every wash and wear. It is, by any honest definition, a fossil-fuel product. 🟡 Some newer alternatives use plant content (cactus, apple, pineapple) but are still blended with plastic binders — meaning they're partially bio-based, but not biodegradable. 🟢 Truly bio-based leather — like Green Sapio's — uses 100% agricultural-sourced raw materials (wheat crop & rice straw), is chemical-free, plastic-free, and fully biodegradable end-to-end. Not just in name. In practice. The distinction matters enormously for brands navigating ESG reporting, EU compliance, and consumer trust. Calling PU leather "sustainable" in 2025 is the same as calling a plastic bag "eco-friendly" because it's reusable once. The bar has moved. If your brand is evaluating material switches, make sure you're asking the right questions: ✅ Is it bio-based from seed to shelf? ✅ Is it plastic-free? ✅ Is it independently verified? ✅ Does it biodegrade fully — not just partially? Green Sapio answers yes to all four. What questions do you have about bio-based materials? Drop them below — I'll answer each one personally. #VeganLeather #Greenwashing #SustainableFashion #BioBased #ESG #CircularEconomy #GreenSapio #SustainableMaterials
