Why Unstructured Summer Time Is Actually Great For Kids 🧠
"Boredom is not the enemy. Here's what the science says."
Caption
Here's something counterintuitive: letting your child be "bored" this summer might be the best thing you do for them. 🌿

When children have unscheduled time, they:
✅ Develop creativity and problem-solving
✅ Learn to self-regulate emotions
✅ Build imaginative play skills
✅ Become more independent

The trick? Mix free time with gentle structured activities — like what we do at BSFC Summer Camp. 💛

Balance is the magic word this summer.

Caption
School starts soon. But the prep starts now — and it doesn't have to feel like studying. 📚✨

1️⃣ Establish a sleep & wake routine now

4️⃣ Encourage independent eating & dressing

🎨 Overall Visual Identity — May

  • Primary palette: Bright yellow (sun), sky blue, coral orange — full summer energy
  • Secondary: mint green for school-prep content; lavender/violet for emotional/wrap content
  • Fonts: Chunky playful display (Nunito Black / Fredoka) for headings; clean sans for body
  • Avoid: Cold blues, corporate greys, anything that looks like a formal school brochure
  • Every creative should feel like summer — warm, bright, joyful

📐 Format Specs for Designers

  • Feed posts: 1080×1080px (1:1) — primary
  • Reels cover: 1080×1350px (4:5) — for feed thumbnail
  • Stories: 1080×1920px (9:16) — always export a story version for key posts
  • Reel video: 1080×1920px, 25–45 secs, subtitles on for silent viewers
  • Carousels: Up to 10 slides — keep each slide text minimal, one idea per slide
